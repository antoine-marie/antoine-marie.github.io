Hello!

I'm a second year master student at the École Normale Supérieure de Lyon, where I follow the "Computational Physics and Chemistry" course programme.

Currently, I'm doing an internship in Pierre-François Loos' research group at the 'Laboratoire de Chimie et Physique Quantique' in Toulouse, France. This internship is part of the ERC PTEROSOR project.
My research interest is theoretical chemistry and, in particular, electronic structure theory. My current work deals with the solution structure of the coupled-cluster equations. We want to explore the interconnections between these solutions in the complex plane and the physical meaning, or unphysical, of them.

Projects

Higher roots of the coupled-cluster equations

Prior to my second year Master degree internship, I did a bibliographical report about alternative formulations of single-reference coupled-cluster and the higher roots of the coupled-cluster equations.
I'm currently doing a six months internship using this report as a starting point. The aim of this internship is to investigate the physical meaning of non-standard solutions of the alternative coupled-cluster formulations presented in the aforementioned report. In particular, we are interested in the possible description of excited states using these non-standard coupled-cluster solutions.

Perturbation theory in the complex plane

As part of the first year of my Master degree, I did a three months internship under the supervision of Pierre-François Loos. During this internship, we explored the links between the position of the energy singularities in the complex plane and the behavior of the energy perturbation series expansion.
